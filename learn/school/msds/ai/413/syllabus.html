MSDS 413: Time Series Analysis & Forecasting
Instructor: Jamie Riggs, Ph.D.

Description: The objective of this course is to cover key analytical techniques used in the analysis and forecasting of time series data. Specific topics include the role of forecasting in organiza- tions, exponential smoothing methods, stationary and non-stationary time series, autocor- relation and partial autocorrelation functions, univariate autoregressive integrated moving average (ARIMA) models, seasonal models, Box-Jenkins methodology, regression models with ARIMA errors, transfer function modeling, intervention analysis, and multivariate time series analysis.

- Learn the fundamental analytical tools/concepts used in time series analysis. These include: Autocorrelation and partial autocorrelation functions, Strict and weak stationarity, Transformations such as log and differencing, Exponential smoothing models, Long memory models

- Learn the analytical framework used in the study of ARIMA models including: Formulation, Autocorrelation and partial autocorrelation functions, Stationarity requirements, Differencing, Seasonality, Forecasting

- Develop an understanding of volatility models: ARCH models, GARCH models

- Develop an Understanding of multivariate time series models: Cross-covariance matrix, VAR models, VARMA models
